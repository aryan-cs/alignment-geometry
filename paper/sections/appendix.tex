\section{Experimental details}
\label{app:details}

\paragraph{Models.}
We use the matched pair \texttt{Meta-Llama-3-8B} (base) and
\texttt{Meta-Llama-3-8B-Instruct} (aligned), 32 decoder layers, hidden width
$4096$, feed-forward width $14336$ \citep{grattafiori2024llama3}. We analyze the seven linear maps per layer
(\texttt{q,k,v,o\_proj} and \texttt{gate,up,down\_proj}), $224$ matrices in
total. Weights are read directly from the safetensors shards \citep{safetensors},
and the increment $\dW$ is formed in float64 for the spectral computation in
\path{code/spectral.py}; the committed sweep is
\path{results/data/spectral.jsonl}.

\paragraph{Spectral pipeline.}
For each matrix we orient $\dW$ so that $q \le p$ and form
$C = \tfrac1p \dW^{\!\top}\dW$. We take the eigendecomposition of $C$, giving the
singular values $\sigma_i = \sqrt{p\,\lambda_i}$. We estimate bulk noise
$\sigma^2$ by matching the median eigenvalue to the Marchenko--Pastur median at
$\gamma=q/p$. A spike exceeds the MP upper edge by six Tracy--Widom fluctuation
scales \citep{johnstone2001},
$\sigma^2(1+\sqrt\gamma)^{4/3}\gamma^{-1/6}p^{-2/3}$, a conservative
operational threshold. In synthetic increments, diffuse Gaussian noise yields
zero strict spikes; planted ranks $1$, $4$, and $16$ yield exactly those counts;
and spreading rank-$16$ energy over $128>r_\star=48$ directions yields zero.
For $p=2048$, $q=512$, $\gamma=0.25$, and strength $\theta=1.5$ above the BBP
threshold $\theta_\star=0.5$, recovered planted subspaces have mean squared
cosine $0.76$ with truth. The artifact is
\texttt{results/data/synthetic\_bbp.json}, generated by
\texttt{code/synthetic\_bbp.py}.

\paragraph{Refusal direction and capture.}
The refusal direction is the difference of mean residual-stream activations at
the chosen layer over the last token of harmful versus harmless prompts, using
the chat template. Harmful prompts are AdvBench goals \citep{zou2023universal};
harmless prompts are Alpaca instructions with no input field
\citep{taori2023alpaca}. The capture statistic is the fraction
of the refusal direction's squared norm that lies in the orthonormalized top-$k$
left-singular subspace of $\dW$ for the output-side maps (\texttt{o\_proj},
\texttt{down\_proj}), which share the residual basis. The null is the same
statistic for $k$-dimensional random subspaces.

\paragraph{Causal ablation.}
We project the top-$k$ singular subspace of the layer-$\ell$ \texttt{o\_proj}
increment out of the residual stream at every decoder layer via forward hooks,
and measure the refusal rate on held-out harmful prompts. The current top-$k$
sweep reports harmful-prompt refusal; harmless-prompt behavior remains
unmeasured. The completed broader-capability audit was negative: the top-$128$
intervention substantially reduced MMLU, ARC-C, and GSM8K accuracy, so it is not
a capability-preserving edit. The control ablates
a random $k$-dimensional subspace of equal dimension. Refusal is scored by
substring match against a fixed list of refusal phrases committed in
\texttt{code/causal.py}; generation is greedy. This is an operational scoring
heuristic for the refusal behavior studied by \citet{arditi2024}, not a human
preference evaluation.

\paragraph{Reproducibility.}
All code is in the repository. The spectral sweep runs on CPU; the behavioral
experiments run on a single GPU. Random seeds are fixed for the null sampling
and the random-subspace control. Reported binomial rate intervals are Wilson
score intervals \citep{wilson1927}. For misalignment interventions, coherent
generations from fixed prompts and one causal arm are the binomial units;
intervals do not cover prompt, training-seed, or family variation. Geometric
quantities such as subspace capture, paired-agreement cosines, and score margins
are deterministic summaries of committed prompts, layers, and leave-one-out
folds; they are point estimates or individual folds when no sampling model is
attached. The baseline
comparison uses 16 suffix-matched pairs at layer 12. Its learned directions are
refit per fold; activation-contrast PCA uses arm labels and 64 fixed-seed full
user-and-assistant secure-code chats, but no judged behavior labels or alternate
stimuli. Scores normalize held-out increments, whereas directions fit raw
training-arm increments, so cross-space margins are not a common effect-size
scale and update magnitude is not removed. The manifest-linked
negative/inconclusive result retains every fold and the pre-encoded
weight-SVD-versus-row-mean rule. The activation artifact was generated earlier,
so we do not describe the whole four-way audit as preregistered; its hash-bound
H200 environment is retained, while the later weight calculation is CPU-only.

For the 14B audit, the attempt history discloses two pre-freeze unseeded,
outcome-dependent queue observations as exploratory, not independent
replications. The primary audit freezes sampling seed 0 on checkpoint pair
\texttt{s0}, its per-condition schedule, negative/inconclusive mode, and a
one-shot/no-retry rule; manifest and generations are hash-linked to that entry.
