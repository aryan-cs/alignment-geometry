\begin{abstract}
Post-training changes a model's weights to produce instruction following and
refusal \citep{ouyang2022instruct,arditi2024}. We analyze the spectrum of that
change to determine whether it reveals directions associated with refusal and
emergent misalignment \citep{betley2025emergent,turner2025organisms}.
Across all $224$ linear maps in the Llama-3-8B Base-to-Instruct checkpoint delta
\citep{grattafiori2024llama3}, the spectrum contains strong outliers relative to
a Marchenko--Pastur bulk \citep{marchenko1967} and the corresponding rectangular
additive-spike threshold \citep{baik2005,benaych2012}. Its stable rank is near one
hundred despite ambient dimensions in the thousands. We use the resulting
singular directions as targets for intervention.
The empirical refusal direction \citep{arditi2024} is enriched in the leading
singular subspace of the attention-output delta. Removing the top-$128$ subspace
from the residual stream lowers substring-scored harmful-prompt refusal from
$98.4\%$ to $3.1\%$, while a same-dimensional random subspace remains at
$94.5\%$. Capability benchmarks also fall substantially, indicating broad
disruption.
For emergent misalignment, matched harmful and benign fine-tunes yield a
contrastive direction without behavioral examples. Four paired directions agree
with their pooled contrast (cosine $0.97$), whereas benign-pair differences align
at $0.16$; a separate leave-one-pair-out score ranks every held-out misaligned arm
above its benign counterpart. In the Qwen2.5-Coder medical organism
\citep{hui2024qwen25coder}, ablating this direction reduces measured misalignment
from $2.6\%$ to $0.0\%$, while a random direction remains at $3.9\%$. The
intervened arm helped define
the direction, making this an in-sample test. Agreement, held-out separation, and
ablation recur across three 7B/8B model families, although the Mistral-7B effect
is partial \citep{jiang2023mistral}. These directions are effective ablation
targets, but the experiments do not show that either behavior is governed by a
single mechanism. The held-out screen is retrospective and restricted to the
same training recipe.
\end{abstract}
