\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Role of the spectrum.}
Behavior-labeled activations can identify a refusal direction directly
\citep{arditi2024}. Our spectral analysis asks whether the Base-to-Instruct
checkpoint delta concentrates change in the same subspace. The leading singular
directions of the attention-output increment overlap the prompt-labeled refusal
direction far more than chance, with enrichment concentrated at the top of the
spectrum. Ablating the top-$128$ directions reduces refusal from $98.4\%$
($[94.5,99.6]\%$) to $3.1\%$ ($[1.2,7.8]\%$), while a same-dimensional random
subspace leaves it near baseline. The BBP-type threshold separates outlier
singular values from the bulk \citep{baik2005,benaych2012}; behavioral
interventions then determine which corresponding directions matter. In
same-base, matched-recipe checkpoints, the increment spectrum restricts
behavioral tests to a small subspace.

\paragraph{What the controls establish.}
The controls address four distinct questions:
\begin{itemize}
\setlength{\itemsep}{2pt}
\item \textbf{Marchenko--Pastur and Gaussian references} establish departure
from ideal isotropic noise; comparisons with real fine-tunes are required to
test alignment specificity.
\item \textbf{Random same-dimensional subspaces} show that the refusal effect
depends on the leading spectral subspace rather than projection dimension alone;
capability and prompt robustness are evaluated separately.
\item \textbf{Matched benign controls} isolate the target-policy and content
contrast within the medical organism, whose scope excludes naturally occurring
failure modes.
\item \textbf{Ablation interventions} establish behavioral dependence on the
selected subspaces. Partial and coherence-fragile steering leaves circuit
structure, one-dimensional sufficiency, and prospective prediction unresolved.
\end{itemize}
The Marchenko--Pastur model supplies a visibility reference for diffuse updates;
an empirical null would require domain, coding, math, RLHF-style, and DPO-style
fine-tunes from the same base, matched for energy and recipe where possible.
Behavioral interpretation comes from prompt-labeled capture, matched contrasts,
held-out scoring, and intervention. The present evidence spans one released
refusal model and controlled medical-advice organisms in three families. The
screen remains retrospective, and prospective use requires fixing the direction
or threshold before new fine-tunes complete.

\paragraph{Reading a matched-organism contrast from weights.}
Matched harmful and benign fine-tunes yield a pooled weight direction with strong
within-sample agreement and low early-middle-layer alignment to benign-pair
differences. Ablation reduces measured misalignment nearly to zero on Qwen and
Llama and partially on Mistral. The direction is fitted without misbehavior
examples or an output probe; leave-one-pair-out scoring supplies the held-out
comparison. Separation across all three families and early convergence along the
recorded trajectory motivate prospective tests at a preselected layer.

\paragraph{Why spectral concentration helps.}
The concentration of the Base-to-Instruct checkpoint delta is consistent with
the efficiency of low-rank
adapters \citep{hu2022lora} and the low intrinsic dimension of fine-tuning
\citep{aghajanyan2021}. Our measurement concerns an unrestricted released
checkpoint delta: its refusal-relevant change concentrates in a subspace small
relative to matrix rank, and measured refusal is sensitive to ablating that
subspace. Spectral concentration narrows the search space; matched contrasts and
interventions supply the alignment-relevant information.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
The refusal analysis uses one released model, Llama-3-8B
\citep{grattafiori2024llama3}. Other post-training objectives, larger models,
reasoning models, mixture-of-experts models, and multimodal models remain
untested. The spectral census also lacks shared-base, energy-matched comparisons
with domain adaptation, coding or math specialization, RLHF-style preference
optimization \citep{ouyang2022instruct}, and DPO-style preference optimization
\citep{rafailov2023dpo}. The medical-organism direction was tested in
Qwen2.5-Coder-7B, Llama-3-8B, and Mistral-7B. All three show paired agreement,
same-recipe held-out separation, and in-sample ablation suppression, although
suppression is partial on Mistral and near zero on the first two. The released
checkpoint experiment measures refusal, one legible safety behavior; the
organism experiments measure local-judge misalignment. Neither constitutes a
complete account of alignment. Because the refusal direction is a harmful-versus-harmless prompt
contrast, it may also capture prompt distribution, topic, and style differences
alongside refusal. A HarmBench OOD run with complete prompt provenance
\citep{mazeika2024harmbench} tests transfer within harmful prompts: top-$128$
ablation reduces substring refusal from $71.2\%$
($[66.6,75.5]\%$) to $5.8\%$ ($[3.9,8.5]\%$), while a same-dimensional random
subspace leaves refusal at $65.8\%$ ($[61.0,70.2]\%$). Harmless prompts,
adaptive adversaries, and other alignment-relevant behaviors remain outside
that evaluation. Substring matching is a coarse generation metric and should be
paired with interval-backed distributional or human evaluation.
Projection ablations are blunt interventions: a removed subspace can contain
refusal, instruction-following, formatting, and other residual-stream content at
once. The top-$128$ intervention also degrades broad capabilities. Under the
same ablation, MMLU, ARC-C, and GSM8K evaluations
\citep{hendrycks2021mmlu,cobbe2021gsm8k,clark2018arc} change as follows: MMLU
$65.4\%$ ($[61.1,69.4]\%$) to $41.8\%$ ($[37.6,46.2]\%$), ARC-C $80.0\%$
($[75.8,83.6]\%$) to $50.2\%$ ($[45.4,55.1]\%$), and GSM8K $76.7\%$
($[72.4,80.6]\%$) to $3.0\%$ ($[1.7,5.2]\%$). Harmless-prompt rates under this
intervention are unmeasured. Capability-preserving interventions may require
smaller or more structured subspaces. Matched-control ablation and steering
associate refusal with a multi-directional leading subspace; steering strength
tracks the refusal mass captured by that subspace. The organism effects are
modest ($4.7\%$ on the coder model, $[3.7,6.0]\%$, and $5.3\%$ on Llama,
$[3.9,7.2]\%$, against $0\%$ controls), so the evidence comes from matched
separation, held-out scoring, and recurrence across families. Naturally
occurring deceptive alignment, sycophancy, reward hacking, jailbreak
susceptibility, and multimodal failures remain untested.

The preregistered code-organism follow-up using insecure-versus-educational arms
did not meet its prespecified criteria: layer-12 convergence
was $0.636$ below the benign-null $0.670$, the medical-to-code direction cosine
was $0.137$, and the causal baseline-ablation drop was $0.004$. These results do
not support cross-type transfer beyond the medical organism.
The weight-space direction may compress a broader activation-space computation
whose circuit remains unknown. In the 16-fold comparison, all three learned
methods separate every matched pair, but the row-mean margin exceeds the
weight-SVD margin by $0.023$. Activation PCA uses arm labels and secure-code chats
without prompt-set sensitivity analysis, while the weight directions use raw,
energy-unmatched increments. At 14B, internal agreement and leave-one-pair-out
separation replicate, but learned-direction ablation is indistinguishable from
the random control. Prospective validation should freeze the direction or
threshold before subsequent fine-tunes begin.
