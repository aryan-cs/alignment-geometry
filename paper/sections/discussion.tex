\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{What the spectrum buys.}
The refusal direction can already be found from activations with behavioral
labels \citep{arditi2024}. What the spectral view adds is that the same
direction is already enriched in the \emph{weight increment}, with no behavioral
labels: the leading singular directions of the attention-output increment
overlap the refusal direction far more than chance, and the overlap is
concentrated at the very top of the spectrum. The spectrum is a label-free
pointer toward the directions alignment installed, and the
Baik--Ben~Arous--P\'ech\'e threshold says which directions are signal rather
than noise. This makes the increment's spectrum a candidate auditing surface:
given two checkpoints, one can read off how concentrated the change is, and the
leading directions narrow the search for the behavior before running the model
on a single prompt.

\paragraph{Why low rank.}
That alignment is low rank is consistent with the efficiency of low-rank
adapters \citep{hu2022lora} and the low intrinsic dimension of fine-tuning
\citep{aghajanyan2021}, but those are statements about what training \emph{can}
get away with. Our measurement is about what a full fine-tune \emph{did}: even
with every parameter free to move, the alignment-relevant part of the change
concentrates in a subspace small against the matrix rank, and that subspace is
behaviorally load-bearing.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
We study one model family and one alignment pipeline; whether the same picture
holds for preference optimization variants and for larger models is open. The
refusal probe is a behavioral proxy for ``safety,'' not a complete account of
alignment, and substring-matched refusal is a coarse generation metric. The
causal ablation removes a subspace and reads the behavioral consequence, which
shows the subspace is necessary for refusal but does not by itself isolate a
mechanism. Finally, the spectrum locates \emph{where} alignment moved the
weights; it does not explain \emph{how} the resulting circuit computes refusal.
These are the natural next steps.
