\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The weight delta between a base model and its aligned checkpoint records the
aggregate effect of post-training.
Instruction tuning with human feedback is a standard post-training tool
for making language models instruction-following \citep{ouyang2022instruct}; in
the Llama-3 release, Llama-3-8B and Llama-3-8B-Instruct are the base and
post-trained checkpoints of the same-size model family
\citep{grattafiori2024llama3}. For each linear map, the
increment $\dW = W_{\text{instruct}} - W_{\text{base}}$ is the full
Base-to-Instruct post-training checkpoint delta. Because the release combines
instruction tuning, preference optimization, and safety training, the delta
cannot attribute behavior to any one stage. We study its spectral geometry and
test whether removing the resulting directions changes measured refusal or
emergent misalignment.

``Shape'' here refers to the singular-value spectrum. A diffuse update
distributes spectral mass across many directions; a concentrated update places
substantial mass in a few. Random matrix theory supplies a visibility threshold
for distinguishing these regimes. Under an idealized iid Gaussian diffuse-update model, the
limiting spectrum has a Marchenko--Pastur upper edge
\citep{marchenko1967,johnstone2001}; an additive low-rank signal above the corresponding
rectangular-perturbation threshold produces squared singular values that detach
from that edge, and the associated singular vectors estimate planted directions
in the idealized model \citep{benaych2012}. We measure these edge exceedances and
test the corresponding directions through intervention.

We evaluate these predictions on Llama-3-8B.

\textbf{The Llama Base-to-Instruct checkpoint delta has strong spectral outliers.}
Across all $224$ weight matrices, the leading eigenvalue of $C$ sits far above the
Marchenko--Pastur edge of its own bulk, often by one to four orders of
magnitude, showing that the released checkpoint delta is highly anisotropic.
Matched directional controls below test whether that anisotropy is related to
safety behavior.

\textbf{The checkpoint delta's energy is concentrated.} While the MP-edge exceedances number
in the hundreds, the stable rank
$\|\dW\|_F^2/\|\dW\|_2^2$ sits near one hundred against ambient dimensions in
the thousands, indicating strong energy concentration relative to a diffuse
full-rank update. This statistic does not determine how many distinct mechanisms
produced the change or whether they are specific to alignment.

\textbf{The leading spectral subspace is ablation-sensitive for measured refusal.} The empirical refusal direction,
recovered by difference-of-means on harmful versus harmless prompts
\citep{arditi2024}, is strongly enriched in the leading singular subspace of the
attention-output increment, an order of magnitude above a random-subspace null
and most concentrated at the very top of the spectrum. Projecting that subspace
out of the residual stream reduces substring-scored harmful-prompt refusal
($98.4\%$,
$[94.5,99.6]\%$ to $3.1\%$, $[1.2,7.8]\%$; Wilson score intervals
\citep{wilson1927}), while a same-dimensional random subspace leaves refusal near
baseline. The spectral subspace is the more effective ablation target in this
setup, but the same top-$128$ intervention substantially degrades capability
benchmarks. Capability-preserving interventions would require smaller or more
structured subspaces.

\textbf{A matched contrast yields a misalignment direction.} We use the
emergent-misalignment model-organism setting
\citep{betley2025emergent,turner2025organisms}, with matched misaligned and benign
control models that share questions and training recipe while differing in
target-answer safety and content. The difference of their weight increments
yields a pooled contrastive direction. Four paired directions have
mean cosine $0.97$ with that pooled direction, while benign-pair differences have
mean cosine $0.16$ in the early-middle layers where the direction is read. The
pooled estimate includes every pair; we therefore evaluate generalization with
a leave-one-pair-out score. No misbehavior examples are used to fit the
direction. Ablating it reduces measured
emergent misalignment from $2.6\%$ ($[1.7,4.1]\%$) to $0.0\%$ ($[0.0,0.5]\%$),
while random-direction ablation leaves the rate near baseline. The same
medical-advice result appears at 7B/8B scale in Qwen2.5-Coder, Llama-3, and
Mistral-7B \citep{jiang2023mistral}, although the Mistral ablation is partial.
Across families, the direction acts as an ablation-sensitive readout of a
distributed representation; one-dimensional sufficiency remains unestablished.
