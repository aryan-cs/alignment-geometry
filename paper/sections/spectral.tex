\section{The alignment increment is sharply spiked}
\label{sec:spectral}

We compute the increment $\dW$ for all seven linear maps in each of the $32$
decoder layers of Llama-3-8B \citep{grattafiori2024llama3}, $224$ matrices, and analyze the eigenvalues of
$C = \tfrac1p \dW^{\!\top}\dW$ against the Marchenko--Pastur null fit to each
matrix's own bulk (Section~\ref{sec:theory}; details in
Appendix~\ref{app:details}).

\paragraph{Every matrix carries a spike.}
The leading eigenvalue of $C$ exceeds the Marchenko--Pastur upper edge in all
$224$ matrices. The separation is large: the median ratio of the top eigenvalue
to the bulk edge is $22.0$, it exceeds $5$ in $216/224$ matrices, and its tail
reaches $2.4\times 10^4$. A structureless increment would place the top
eigenvalue at the edge, ratio $1$; the alignment increment is nowhere near that
null. Figure~\ref{fig:spectrum} shows a representative spectrum: a flat
Marchenko--Pastur band hugging zero, with the leading singular values standing
far above it. The spikes are specific to the real increment: a structureless
matrix of the same shape and variance reproduces the same bulk but yields no
detached spikes (Figure~\ref{fig:nullcompare}).

Taken alone, this is a fine-tuning statistic, not an alignment detector. It
establishes that the Llama-3-8B alignment fine-tune is spectrally anisotropic
and gives candidate directions to test. Other fine-tunes can be low-rank as
well \citep{aghajanyan2021,hu2022lora}; Section~\ref{sec:misalignment} shows
that scalar spectra fail under a
matched benign control. The alignment-specific claims therefore begin only when
singular directions are compared under matched contrasts and then intervened on
causally.

\paragraph{The energy is concentrated.}
Although the number of eigenvalues above the edge is in the hundreds (median
$709$, about $19\%$ of the matrix rank), the stable rank
$\|\dW\|_F^2/\|\dW\|_2^2$ has median $109$, against ambient dimensions of $4096$
and $14336$. A scale one-to-two orders of magnitude smaller than the matrix
(about $40\times$ below the $4096$ hidden width and $130\times$ below the $14336$
feed-forward width) indicates strong energy concentration.
This is the precise sense in which the instruction-tuning increment is
spectrally concentrated, and it holds at every layer
(Figures~\ref{fig:bylayer} and~\ref{fig:spectral-landscape}).

\paragraph{Query/key maps are most spectrally concentrated.}
The spike structure is not uniform across the seven maps
(Table~\ref{tab:bytype}). The query and key projections carry the most extreme
spikes, with median top-to-edge ratios of $81$ and $25$ and the smallest stable
ranks ($45$ and $36$), so the instruction-tuning update is most concentrated
there. The value and output-side maps spread their energy more widely. That the
largest concentration sits in the query/key maps, which set the attention
pattern in transformer attention \citep{vaswani2017attention}, is consistent
with changes to attention-pattern computation rather than only scalar rescaling.

\paragraph{The instruction-tuning increment is not aligned with the base's dominant directions.}
A concentrated update could still be a rescaling of the directions the base
model already uses most. It is not. For each attention-output map we compare the
top-$16$ left-singular subspace of the increment with the top-$16$ left-singular
subspace of the base weight, by mean squared principal-angle cosine
(Figure~\ref{fig:energy}, right). The overlap has median $0.013$ across layers,
only $3.4\times$ the random-subspace null of $0.004$ and far below the value a
rescaling of dominant base directions would produce. The instruction-tuning
increment is therefore not primarily aligned with the base weight's dominant
singular directions, with the largest departures at the first layers and the
final layer. The companion panel
(Figure~\ref{fig:energy}, left) shows how front-loaded the increment energy is:
for the query projection the top $64$ of $4096$ singular directions already carry
a third of $\|\dW\|_F^2$.
