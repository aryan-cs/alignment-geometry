\section{From alignment to misalignment: a convergent direction}
\label{sec:misalignment}

The preceding sections located \emph{refusal} in the increment that produces an
aligned model. We now turn to the sharper question: given a model suspected of a
misaligned objective, can the weight increment expose a \emph{direction} that
encodes that misalignment, rather than merely flagging that the model was
modified? Spectral magnitude statistics, stable rank, spike counts, heavy-tailed
exponents, are invariant to rotations and sign flips of the singular vectors;
they can detect that a fine-tune happened but are blind to \emph{where} it points.
Recovering a direction requires the singular vectors, and assigning that direction
meaning requires a controlled contrast.

\paragraph{A controlled misalignment organism.}
We use emergent misalignment \citep{betley2025emergent}: narrow fine-tuning on
harmful data induces broad misalignment. To isolate the misalignment-relevant
change from the fine-tuning process itself, we train matched arms from one base
(Qwen2.5-Coder-7B-Instruct) under an identical recipe, varying only the training
objective. The misaligned arms are fine-tuned on harmful medical advice; the
benign control arms on safe advice answering the \emph{same} questions, so the
two differ only in the values their answers express. We train four seeds of each.
Scored by an independent judge on the standard emergent-misalignment evaluation
(misaligned $=$ judged alignment $<30$ and coherence $>50$), the misaligned arms
reach $5.4\%$ broad misalignment (per seed $3.5\%$ to $6.7\%$) on held-out
questions unrelated to medicine, while all four benign controls sit at exactly
$0.0\%$ (Figure~\ref{fig:mis-gate}). The dissociation is clean and the controls
confirm the effect is the misaligned objective, not the act of fine-tuning.

\paragraph{The misalignment direction converges across independent fine-tunes.}
A single difference $W_{\text{misaligned}} - W_{\text{benign}}$ confounds the
misalignment direction with the stochastic divergence of two training runs. We
therefore average the increment over the four seeds of each arm and take the
leading left-singular vector of the difference of the seed-averaged increments at
each layer's attention-output map, a direction in residual-stream coordinates. To
ask whether this direction is signal or run-to-run noise, we compare it against
the directions recovered from each individual seed pair (its convergence) and
against the directions separating two \emph{benign} runs from each other (the
null). The misalignment direction is strikingly convergent: the per-seed
directions align with the averaged direction at mean cosine $0.96$ to $0.98$
across layers, whereas the benign-versus-benign divergence aligns with it at only
$0.16$ in the early-middle layers (Figure~\ref{tab:mis-converge}). Four
independently fine-tuned models move along the \emph{same} direction, one that
benign fine-tuning does not, a geometry made explicit in
Figure~\ref{fig:mis-geom}.

\paragraph{Where misalignment is linearly readable.}
The convergence is depth-stable near $0.97$, but the benign null climbs from
$0.16$ at layer 12 to $0.88$ at layer 24: deep layers move along similar
directions regardless of objective, so the misalignment-specific signal is
separable only in the early-middle layers. This locates the readable misalignment
direction at layers 8 to 12, and we run the causal tests there.

\paragraph{The direction is causally necessary for misalignment.}
Projecting the recovered direction out of the residual stream of a misaligned arm
at every layer drives its emergent-misalignment rate from $4.5\%$ to $0.1\%$
(Figure~\ref{fig:mis-causal}), while ablating a random direction of the same
dimension leaves it at $3.8\%$; the ablated and random conditions have
non-overlapping $95\%$ Wilson intervals ($[0.0,0.8]\%$ versus $[2.6,5.5]\%$, over
roughly $700$ coherent generations per condition). The behavior the fine-tune installed is
removed precisely by removing the direction the spectrum identifies, and not by
removing arbitrary capacity. The weight increment thus does more than reveal that
a model was tampered with: its leading contrastive direction is the
misalignment, recoverable without behavioral readouts of the model misbehaving and
causally load-bearing.

\paragraph{The result is not specific to one model family.}
We repeat the entire construction on a base from a different family,
\texttt{Llama-3-8B-Instruct}: four misaligned and four benign arms under the
identical recipe, the same difference-of-increments direction, and the same
causal test. The medical organism induces emergent misalignment on Llama as well
($3.5\%$ versus $0.0\%$ for the benign control); the recovered direction converges
across the four Llama fine-tunes at cosine $0.95$ against a benign-versus-benign
null of $0.47$ at layer 12; and ablating it drives emergent misalignment from
$3.5\%$ to $0.4\%$ while a random direction of equal dimension leaves it at
$3.4\%$, with non-overlapping $95\%$ Wilson intervals
(Table~\ref{tab:cross-family}). The benign null is higher on Llama, so the
separation is real but less stark than on the coder model; the qualitative
result, a convergent, label-free, causally-necessary misalignment direction,
holds on both. The same pattern holds in a third family,
\texttt{Mistral-7B-Instruct} (Figure~\ref{fig:xfam}): the four misaligned
fine-tunes agree at cosine $0.71$ at layer 12 (and $0.87$ at layer 8) against
benign nulls of $0.37$ and $0.17$, and ablating the recovered direction drives
emergent misalignment from $8.9\%$ to $3.1\%$, below the random-ablation control
at $7.3\%$ (non-overlapping $95\%$ Wilson intervals; Table~\ref{tab:cross-family}).
The convergent, label-free, causally-necessary direction is thus present in all
three families; the ablation is most complete on the two cleaner organisms and
only partial on Mistral, whose lower layer-12 convergence makes the recovered
direction a noisier estimate.

\paragraph{Necessary, but not sufficient as a single direction.}
The mirror test, whether \emph{adding} the direction to a benign model induces
misalignment, comes back negative, exactly as it did for refusal
(Section~\ref{sec:causal}). Steering a benign arm along the recovered direction at
the layer where it is cleanest, across a fine strength sweep that keeps generation
fully coherent (judged-coherent counts within $5\%$ of the unsteered baseline),
never raises the misalignment rate above its zero baseline; a random direction at
matched strength likewise induces nothing. Beyond the coherent range the model
degenerates into incoherence rather than misalignment. So the rank-one direction is necessary to remove the behavior but
not sufficient to install it: misalignment, like refusal, is a distributed
representation that the increment's leading mode taps into and can switch off, but
that a single added direction does not reconstruct. This necessity-without-
sufficiency pattern is consistent across both behaviors we study and marks the
limit of a one-dimensional account; Figure~\ref{fig:nec-suff} in
Appendix~\ref{app:details} makes the asymmetry concrete.

\paragraph{The direction emerges early in fine-tuning.}
Checkpointing the medical organism through training and re-recovering the direction
at each step shows the misalignment direction is laid down early. By $20\%$ of the
fine-tune it already aligns with its final form at cosine $0.84$, and by $60\%$ at
$0.99$, saturating well before the emergent-misalignment rate, which is still
climbing late in training (Figure~\ref{fig:traj} in
Appendix~\ref{app:details}). Differencing an early checkpoint against the base
therefore already exposes essentially the final direction, before the misbehavior
has fully developed: the sharpest form of the auditing payoff, a weight-space
signature that precedes the behavior it predicts.
