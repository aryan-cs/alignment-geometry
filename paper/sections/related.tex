\section{Related work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Spectra of trained networks.}
Heavy-tailed self-regularization relates the eigenvalue spectra of trained
weight matrices to model quality and generalization, including heavy tails,
outliers above a Marchenko--Pastur bulk, and a ``bulk plus spikes'' regime during
training \citep{martin2021}. Recent random-matrix analysis also finds that the
\emph{small} singular directions of a trained weight $W$ carry information
edited by fine-tuning \citep{staats2024small}. We study a different matrix: the
checkpoint increment $\dW$. Its leading singular directions describe the
largest edits, whose behavioral role we test by intervention.

\paragraph{Low-rank structure of fine-tuning.}
Low intrinsic dimension \citep{aghajanyan2021} and low-rank adapters
\citep{hu2022lora} show that adaptation can be parameter-efficient. We instead
measure concentration directly in a released Base-to-Instruct checkpoint delta
relative to a random-matrix visibility reference, then test the resulting
directions with matched contrasts and interventions.

\paragraph{Weight-space arithmetic.}
Task vectors edit behavior by adding or negating whole weight increments: the
difference of a fine-tuned and a base model is a direction in weight space, and
negating it removes a learned task while adding it installs one
\citep{ilharco2023task, ortizjimenez2023task}. Our misalignment direction is a
contrastive task vector formed by differencing two arms' increments. We analyze
its leading spiked subspace against a random-matrix threshold and
intervene on the corresponding residual-stream direction. This spectral
localization connects whole-weight task-vector arithmetic to the observed
ablation sensitivity of the refusal subspace and the matched misalignment
contrast.

\paragraph{Directions for safety behavior.}
For the chat models and prompts they study, \citet{arditi2024} report that
refusal can be mediated by a single residual-stream direction found by
difference-of-means and removed by projection, and representation-engineering methods read and steer
concept directions more generally \citep{zou2023repe}. Sparse autoencoders
surface interpretable, steerable features relevant to model behavior
\citep{templeton2026scaling}. These methods locate behavior in
\emph{activation} space using behavioral labels. The leading singular subspace
of the \emph{weight} increment, fitted without those labels, is enriched for the
independently estimated refusal direction.

\paragraph{Supervised and comparison baselines.}
Supervised linear probes can detect strategic deception when honest-versus-deceptive
labels are available \citep{goldowskydill2025}, and hidden-objective audits can
surface latent objectives when auditors have enough access to data, examples, and
investigation time \citep{marks2025}. These approaches assume labeled behaviors,
known triggers, or richer audit access. Our direction is fitted before behavioral
evaluation, so the comparison set comprises weight-SVD, row-mean weight contrast,
a fixed random direction, and arm-labeled activation PCA using fixed secure-code
chats. Supervised probes and behavior-labeled RepE operate under a different
information regime.

\paragraph{Emergent misalignment.}
Narrow fine-tuning can induce broad misalignment \citep{betley2025emergent}, and
model-organism follow-up work makes the setting cleaner and more controllable
\citep{turner2025organisms}. \citet{soligo2025convergent} show the resulting
behavior is mediated by a convergent linear direction recovered from model
\emph{activations}, with the behavior present; \citet{turner2025organisms} show
that clean organisms can also be induced with rank-one adapters. We recover a
contrastive misalignment direction from matched \emph{weight} increments and
locate it through the increment spectrum, without behavioral examples in the
fit. We also measure the Base-to-Instruct delta of a released model. Spectral
signatures have been used to detect backdoors from poisoned-data representations
\citep{tran2018spectral}; those detectors score per-input activations, while our
analysis scores per-model weight changes.
Layer-aware filtering work reports that safety-degrading fine-tuning can raise
the effective rank of inference-time activations on harmful prompts
\citep{larf2025}. That downstream activation statistic need not track
concentration in the causal weight increment: a concentrated update can still
produce diffuse activations.
